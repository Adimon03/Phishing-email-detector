## ğŸš© Understanding Red Flags

### Common Red Flags You'll See:

#### 1. Suspicious URLs
**What it means**: The email contains links to fake or dangerous websites

**Example**:
> "Contains 1 suspicious URL with red flags like wrong domains, suspicious TLDs, or IP addresses"

**Why it matters**: Clicking these links could steal your information or install malware

---

#### 2. Sender Issues
**What it means**: The sender's email address looks fake or doesn't match who they claim to be

**Example**:
> "Sender email address has 1 issue indicating potential spoofing or impersonation"

**Why it matters**: Scammers pretend to be trusted companies to trick you

---

#### 3. Urgency Tactics
**What it means**: The email tries to pressure you into acting quickly

**Example**:
> "Uses 4 urgency tactics to pressure you into acting quickly without thinking"

**Why it matters**: Scammers use time pressure to prevent you from thinking clearly

**Look for phrases like**:
- "Act now!"
- "Within 24 hours"
- "Immediate action required"
- "Limited time offer"

---

#### 4. Sensitive Information Requests
**What it means**: The email asks for personal information that companies never request via email

**Example**:
> "Requests 3 types of sensitive information that legitimate companies never ask for via email"

**Why it matters**: Legitimate companies NEVER ask for this information via email

**Never provide via email**:
- Passwords
- Credit card numbers
- Social Security Number
- Bank account details
- PIN codes

---

#### 5. Suspicious Keywords
**What it means**: The email uses words commonly found in scam emails

**Example**:
> "Contains 8 suspicious keywords commonly used in phishing attempts"

**Why it matters**: Scammers use specific language patterns to manipulate you

**Common scam words**:
- "Verify your account"
- "Suspended"
- "Urgent"
- "Congratulations"
- "Winner"
- "Confirm your identity"

## ğŸ’¡ Pro Tips

### 1. Always Verify
Even if an email looks legitimate, verify through official channels:
- Visit the company website directly (don't click email links)
- Call using the number from their official website
- Check your account by logging in directly

### 2. Look for Multiple Red Flags
One red flag might be a mistake, but multiple red flags = scam:
- Suspicious sender + urgent language = likely scam
- Wrong domain + requests for info = definitely scam
- Multiple issues = high confidence it's phishing

### 3. Trust Your Instincts
If something feels wrong, it probably is:
- Too good to be true? It is.
- Unexpected urgency? Suspicious.
- Unusual request? Verify first.

### 4. When in Doubt
- Don't click
- Don't reply
- Don't provide information
- Contact the company directly
